# Auditicle Article Readiness Benchmark 2026 — Methodology

Status: protocol published; results withheld until the target sample is collected and quality checked.

## Research question

How often do public English-language articles expose observable technical, provenance, answer-structure, retrieval, and source-support signals, and how do descriptive readiness scores differ across WordPress, Next.js, Webflow, and other CMS groups?

## Target sample

- 1,000 public article URLs.
- Publicly accessible, indexable article-like pages only.
- English-language pages for the first edition.
- No login, paywall circumvention, private URL, or robots.txt override.
- Deduplicate canonical URLs and hostnames according to the documented sampling plan.
- Record platform as WordPress, Next.js, Webflow, Custom CMS, Other, or Unknown using observable evidence only.

## Primary variables

- Visible author present.
- dateModified signal present.
- Thin server HTML before browser rendering.
- Number of citation/reference links.
- Median Technical SEO, GEO, AEO, RAG retrieval, and LLMO readiness scores.
- Descriptive platform-group comparisons.

## Publication safeguards

Raw URLs, hostnames, raw HTML, article text, IP addresses, and personal data are not included in the public aggregate dataset. Results are not published until the target sample is reached, duplicate and malformed records are removed, and limitations are reviewed. Platform differences are descriptive and must not be presented as causal.
